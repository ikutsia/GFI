Values

The Film Institute of Georgia stands as an institution grounded in principles of justice and transparency, welcoming film professionals who align with its core values. While not a politically affiliated entity, the institute embodies values that hold societal significance. These values include:

  1. Appreciation, preservation, nurturing, and advocacy for Georgian culture as a fundamental national asset.
  2. Upholding the principles of freedom of speech, expression and creativity.
  3. Advocating for equality without discrimination based on various factors such as race, color, sex, age, origin, place of birth, residence, socioeconomic status or title, national, ethnic or social affiliation, profession, marital status, health, disabilities, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, political or other opinion, or any other identifying marker.
  4. Backing the country's territorial integrity amidst ongoing Russian occupation, engaging in constructive dialogue with the population residing in occupied regions, nurturing and supporting common cultural values. At the same time demonstrating special responsibility and consideration for displaced citizens from the occupied territories of Georgia, as well as the families affected by the oppressive regime.
  5. Supporting Georgia's path towards a Euro-Atlantic future.
